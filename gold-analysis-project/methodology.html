Statistical Methods I | Academic Year 2024-25

üìã Executive Summary

This research project employs a mixed-methods approach combining quantitative statistical analysis of historical gold prices with qualitative insights from investor surveys. The study examines gold price behavior over a 33-month period (January 2023 - October 2025) using both descriptive and inferential statistical techniques to evaluate gold's role as an inflation hedge and understand investor attitudes toward gold investment.

üéØ Research Objectives

1

Descriptive Analysis

Characterize gold price distribution and volatility patterns using central tendency measures, dispersion measures, and distribution shape analysis.

2

Trend Analysis

Identify and quantify long-term price trends using linear regression and time-series decomposition techniques.

3

Inflation Hedge Testing

Statistically test the hypothesis that gold prices correlate with inflation using correlation analysis and hypothesis testing.

4

Market Relationships

Examine relationships between gold prices and other economic variables (USD index, interest rates) using multivariate analysis.

5

Investor Attitudes

Assess investor perceptions, investment intentions, and demographic factors through survey-based research.

6

Volatility Dynamics

Analyze how volatility has evolved over time and test for structural breaks in price behavior.
